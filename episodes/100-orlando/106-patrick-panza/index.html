Always Learning

By Andrew Nater

You have to really know something before you can improve it. You can’t just go willy-nilly making and suggesting changes to something you have no experience with. No connection. No investment. Without it, you know nothing.

This is the arcing sentiment that Patrick, our host, holds close.

He’s a cyclist and an urban planner, bridging the gap in understanding between the two communities. It’s tough, particularly in Orlando, a city known for its poor accommodations for pedestrians and cyclists. It is the foundation upon which he will likely make a name for himself, forever affecting the (literal) ins and outs of The City Beautiful.

Amongst the many lessons I’ve taken away from all the great times we’ve covered with Unfold, this one stands out. You have to really spend the time with something to appreciate it, to really speak on it, and potentially change it. It’s the driving reason behind why we seek out locals for our show. Further, we seek out hosts who are advocates for their home and their communities.

Maybe Orlando won’t be the most comfortable or walkable city in the U.S. Maybe it will. Either way, there will always be people here who believe in it. The same can be said for any city in this world. Every city is a city because enough people loved it enough to dig down and settle. Our goal is to find some of those people and see why they did.

When we achieve that goal, it looks like this. This episode is the pinnacle of that ideal. We are still learning and still growing, working out the deepest kinks and foolish mistakes, that’s for sure. But, today, I’m very proud to say we’ve blown past most of that and put together something fantastic.

To my incredible team, to Patrick, and all the other fantastic hosts this season: thank you and congratulations. We all made something beautiful and, in my opinion, honest. For me, that’s all that really counts.
